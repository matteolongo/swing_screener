# Recommended Logic UI Copy

This file contains a shorter, more user-facing version of the `recommended` explanation for onboarding, empty states, helper cards, or modal copy.

## Short Version

`Recommended` does not mean "best-looking stock."

It means the setup passed both layers:

1. the stock looks technically strong
2. the trade also passes risk rules

A stock can rank well and still be `NOT_RECOMMENDED` if the trade structure is weak.

## One-Minute Explanation

The system first looks for strong stocks.

Then it asks a second question:

"Is this a trade worth taking with a clear stop, acceptable risk, and enough upside?"

Only if the answer is yes does the system mark it as `RECOMMENDED`.

## Beginner-Friendly Card Copy

### What `Recommended` Means

This setup is not just strong. It also has:

- an active entry signal
- a valid stop
- controlled risk
- enough potential reward

### What `Not Recommended` Usually Means

The stock may still be interesting, but something important is missing:

- no active signal yet
- stop is unclear
- risk is too high
- reward is too small
- fees are too heavy for the trade

## Tooltip Copy

### Recommended

Passed both quality and risk checks.

### Not Recommended

Interesting idea, but not valid to trade yet under the current rules.

## Daily Review Helper Copy

The Daily Review shows only trades that passed the full checklist.

This helps you focus on setups that are not only strong, but also structured well enough to act on.

## Onboarding Copy

Do not treat every high-scoring stock as a trade.

The platform separates:

- strong stocks
- valid trades

That second filter is what protects capital.

## Empty-State Copy

No recommended trades today.

That does not mean the screener found nothing useful. It means none of the current setups passed the full risk checklist.

Selective trading is part of the strategy.
